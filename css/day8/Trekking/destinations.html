Everest Base Camp: 14 Days of High‑Altitude Wonder

The Everest Base Camp trek is more than a hike — it's a pilgrimage into the heart of the Khumbu. Follow the footsteps of Hillary and Tenzing, fly into the airstrip at Lukla, ascend through rhododendron forests, Buddhist stupas, and glacier moraines. Reach the iconic Base Camp (5,364m) where mountaineers prepare for their summit bid, and witness sunrise over Everest from Kala Patthar (5,545m).

With expert guides, a proven acclimatization schedule, and cozy teahouse lodges, this adventure blends physical challenge with cultural immersion. From the bustling Sherpa capital of Namche Bazaar to the serene Tengboche Monastery, every day unveils staggering views of Everest, Lhotse, Nuptse, and Ama Dablam.

Your Adventure, Day by Day

Everest Base Camp Itinerary

A thoughtfully paced 14-day journey allowing for proper acclimatization and breathtaking mountain views.

Day 1
Arrive in Kathmandu (1,400m) – Airport pickup & transfer to hotel. Evening welcome dinner and trip briefing.
Day 2
Fly to Lukla (2,860m) & trek to Phakding (2,610m) – Scenic 30-min flight to Lukla, then gentle 3-4 hour trek to Phakding along the Dudh Koshi river.
Day 3
Phakding to Namche Bazaar (3,440m) – Cross suspension bridges, enter Sagarmatha National Park. First views of Everest.
Day 4
Acclimatization day in Namche – Hike to Everest View Hotel, visit Sherpa museum, or relax.
Day 5
Namche to Tengboche (3,860m) – Trail through rhododendron forest to famous Tengboche Monastery with Ama Dablam backdrop.
Day 6
Tengboche to Dingboche (4,410m) – Views of Everest, Lhotse & Island Peak. Enter alpine meadow zone.
Day 7
Acclimatization day in Dingboche – Hike to Nagarjun Hill (5,100m) for 360° views.
Day 8
Dingboche to Lobuche (4,940m) – Gradual ascent past memorials of fallen climbers.
Day 9
Lobuche to Gorak Shep (5,164m) – Everest Base Camp (5,364m) – The big day! Trek across Khumbu glacier moraine to Base Camp, then return to Gorak Shep.
Day 10
Kala Patthar sunrise (5,545m) → Pheriche (4,240m) – Early morning ascent to the best viewpoint of Everest, then descend to Pheriche.
Day 11
Pheriche to Namche Bazaar – Retrace trail, celebrate descent through forests.
Day 12
Namche to Lukla – Final walking day, farewell dinner.
Day 13
Fly Lukla to Kathmandu – Scenic flight back, free afternoon. Celebration dinner.
Day 14
Departure – Airport transfer and departure.

Frequently Asked Questions

How difficult is the Everest Base Camp trek?

Strenuous due to altitude (max 5,545m) and trekking 5–7 hours daily. Prior hiking experience and good fitness are essential. Our itinerary includes rest days for proper acclimatization.

Do I need previous high-altitude experience?

Not strictly required, though any experience above 4,000m helps. First-time trekkers with good fitness can complete EBC safely by following our acclimatization plan and listening to the guide.

What is the best time to trek Everest Base Camp?

Spring (March–May) offers blooming rhododendrons and stable weather; autumn (September–November) provides crystal-clear skies. Winter is very cold, summer has monsoon rains.

Can I charge my phone / get Wi-Fi on the trail?

Yes, most teahouses offer solar charging (small fee) and Wi-Fi (paid). But expect limited connectivity in higher villages like Gorak Shep.

What kind of accommodation can I expect?

Basic but cozy teahouses (twin-sharing rooms). Toilets are shared, and hot showers are available for extra cost. We book the best available lodges.

Is travel insurance mandatory?

Absolutely. Your insurance must cover high-altitude trekking (up to 5,500m) and emergency helicopter evacuation. We will verify proof before departure.
