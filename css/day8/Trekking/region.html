The Khumbu Valley

Gateway to the World's Highest Peaks

The Everest region, known locally as Khumbu, is a stunning high-altitude landscape in northeastern Nepal. It is home to four of the world’s six highest mountains: Everest, Lhotse, Makalu, and Cho Oyu. The region is defined by deep gorges, rhododendron forests, and alpine meadows that give way to icy glaciers and rocky moraines.

Beyond the scenery, Khumbu is the spiritual and cultural heartland of the Sherpa people. Centuries-old monasteries, mani stones, and fluttering prayer flags accompany every trail. The region's main gateway is the airstrip at Lukla (2,860m), followed by the famous trekking hub of Namche Bazaar.

Key Areas & Villages

From bustling Namche to remote Gokyo – each corner of the Everest region has its own character.

Namche Bazaar

The gateway to high Khumbu. A vibrant Sherpa town with bakeries, lodges, and the famous Saturday market. Acclimatization hub.

Tengboche Monastery

Spiritual heart of the region, set against Ama Dablam. Witness Buddhist ceremonies and breathtaking mountain views.

Gokyo Valley

Crystal blue lakes, the Ngozumpa Glacier (longest in Nepal), and Gokyo Ri viewpoint – an alternative to crowded EBC.

Everest Base Camp

The iconic trekking destination at 5,364m. Stand at the foot of the world's highest mountain.

Three Passes Trek

For experienced trekkers: Kongma La, Cho La, and Renjo La – high passes offering unrivaled panoramas.

Lukla to Phakding

First day of the classic EBC trek, following the rushing Dudh Koshi river through pine forests.

Living culture

Sherpa People & Buddhist Traditions

The Sherpa people are world-famous for their mountaineering prowess, but their culture runs much deeper. Follow the path of Buddhism: spinning prayer wheels, ancient stupas, and monasteries like Tengboche and Pangboche. The annual Mani Rimdu festival (autumn) features masked dances and rituals.

Visiting a Sherpa home or teahouse offers warm hospitality, butter tea, and stories of Himalayan expeditions. Respect local customs: walk clockwise around mani walls and ask permission before photographing religious sites.

🌿 Flora

Rhododendron forests (spring blooms), pine, birch, juniper, and alpine scrub. Above 4,500m, vegetation gives way to mosses and lichens.

🐾 Fauna

Himalayan tahr, musk deer, snow leopard (rarely seen), red panda, Himalayan monal (national bird), and the elusive yeti legend.

🏞️ National Park

Sagarmatha National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, protects 1,148 sq km of unique ecosystem. Entry permit required for all trekkers.

Plan your season

Best Time to Trek Everest Region

🌸 Spring (Mar–May)

Ideal weather, blooming rhododendrons, stable temperatures. Busy trails but best mountain visibility.

🍂 Autumn (Sep–Nov)

Crystal clear skies, mild days, post-monsoon fresh air. Peak season – book early.

❄️ Winter (Dec–Feb)

Very cold, some lodges closed. Solitude and snow-covered landscapes – for experienced cold-weather trekkers.

☔ Summer/Monsoon (Jun–Aug)

Heavy rain, leeches, cloud cover. Not recommended for high passes, but lower valleys are lush.

Getting to Everest Region

Fly from Kathmandu to Lukla (30 min, scenic) – the most common gateway. Alternatively, fly to Phaplu or take a long jeep drive to Jiri (classic "Pioneer Route"). All treks require two permits: Sagarmatha National Park Entry Permit and Khumbu Pasang Lhamu Rural Municipality Permit. Trekkers also need TIMS card.

Flights to Lukla are weather-dependent; always build buffer days. We arrange all permits and flights as part of your package.

Permits & Fees (2025)

  • Sagarmatha National Park: NPR 3,000 (~USD 25)
  • Khumbu Municipality Permit: NPR 2,000 (~USD 17)
